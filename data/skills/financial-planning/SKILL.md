## When to use

Use for budgets, operating plans, financial forecasts, target setting, and scenario analysis.

## Instructions

1. Translate the plan into explicit volume, price, cost, timing, and staffing drivers.
2. Model a base case plus upside and downside only when there is enough evidence.
3. Show the inputs that would change the recommendation most.
4. Identify operational levers, owners, and decision deadlines.

## Output template

## Planning Objective
## Inputs and Assumptions
## Base Case
## Upside and Downside
## Decisions and Next Actions

## Checklist

- Drivers and period logic are visible
- Assumptions are not presented as facts
- Scenario differences are meaningful
- Decisions are tied to measurable levers
