# Deep Research

Runs a multi-layer research workflow for high-stakes strategy questions by combining market evidence, audience insight, competitor patterns, online signal gathering, confidence scoring, and decision-ready synthesis.

## When to use

Use this skill for strategic work that needs deeper evidence than a normal brief: audience understanding, buying barriers, value drivers, market demand, category education gaps, positioning research, messaging strategy, or any task where the answer should be grounded in multiple evidence streams instead of generic assumptions.

## Context

You are conducting deep research for decision-making, not surface summarization. Your job is to gather, compare, and pressure-test evidence before making recommendations. You should distinguish observed signals, likely interpretations, and strategic recommendations. Be explicit about confidence, uncertainty, and what still needs validation.

## Instructions

## Deep Research Workflow

1. **Frame the decision question**
   - Define the business problem, research goal, and what decision this work should support.
   - State the audience, market, offer, and category context.

2. **Map hypotheses before researching**
   - List likely audience motivations, objections, value drivers, and adoption barriers.
   - Identify what would prove or disprove each hypothesis.

3. **Collect evidence from multiple signal types**
   - Gather audience signals, competitor signals, market/category signals, and educational/behavioral signals.
   - Prefer concrete, observable evidence over generic claims.
   - When online research is available, look for repeated patterns across multiple relevant sources.

4. **Separate facts from interpretation**
   - Explicitly label what is observed, what is inferred, and what remains uncertain.
   - Avoid presenting assumptions as facts.

5. **Synthesize into strategic implications**
   - Translate research into what the client should say, prove, emphasize, de-risk, or explain.
   - Explain why the audience is not acting today and what message changes that behavior.

6. **Build an actionable messaging plan**
   - Define message pillars, proof points, objection-handling angles, audience-specific hooks, and content opportunities.
   - Prioritize the messages by likely business impact.

7. **Document confidence and next steps**
   - Include confidence level, missing evidence, and the next research tasks that would sharpen the conclusion.

## Research Rules
- Do not stop at one source or one angle.
- Use multiple evidence categories whenever possible.
- Be honest when evidence is thin.
- Recommendations must connect directly to evidence.
- Distinguish category education problems from offer-positioning problems.
- Surface the emotional, functional, and economic value the audience is actually buying.

## Expected Output Quality
- Deep, specific, and commercially useful.
- Grounded in evidence and audience logic.
- Clear enough for strategy, messaging, and content teams to act on immediately.
