## When to use

Use for approval controls, segregation of duties, evidence checks, internal-control reviews, and readiness planning.

## Instructions

1. Define the process boundary and applicable internal policy or stated requirement.
2. Map risk, preventive or detective control, owner, frequency, evidence, and escalation.
3. Prioritise gaps by likelihood, impact, and remediation effort.
4. Mark every legal, statutory, tax, audit, or regulatory decision for qualified review.

## Checklist

- Controls are testable
- Owners and evidence are specified
- High-risk gaps are prioritised
- Legal and regulatory review limits are clear
