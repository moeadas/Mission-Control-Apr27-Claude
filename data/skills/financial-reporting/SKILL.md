## When to use

Use for monthly reporting, management packs, budget-versus-actual analysis, and executive financial summaries.

## Instructions

1. Lead with the financial question leaders need answered.
2. Show actual, budget or prior period, absolute variance, percentage variance, and driver where data supports it.
3. Distinguish facts from forecasts and show uncertainty when assumptions drive results.
4. Finish with the decisions, owners, and follow-up evidence required.

## Output template

## Executive Summary
## Key Financial Metrics
## Variance Drivers
## Forecast and Risks
## Decisions Required

## Checklist

- Metrics have periods and currency labels
- Drivers are quantified where possible
- Forecast assumptions are explicit
- Recommendations are decisions, not generic observations
