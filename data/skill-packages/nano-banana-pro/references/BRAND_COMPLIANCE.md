# Brand Identity Compliance Guide

When generating images for clients or brands, always follow this checklist.

---

## Step 1: Extract Brand Elements

Before generating, analyze any reference images provided:

### Color Palette
- Extract dominant colors as hex codes
- Note accent/secondary colors
- Check for brand-specific color gradients
- Note light/dark mode variations

### Typography
- Identify font families (serif, sans-serif, display)
- Note font weights and sizes
- Check for custom/hand-lettered elements
- Look for ALL CAPS vs sentence case patterns

### Layout & Composition
- Grid-based or organic?
- Centered or asymmetric?
- Logo placement zones
- Text-to-image ratio
- Margin/padding patterns

### Visual Style
- Minimalist vs detailed
- Photographic vs illustrated
- Flat vs 3D/rendered
- Dark vs light theme
- Professional vs playful tone

---

## Step 2: Apply Brand Rules

### DO:
- ✅ Use brand hex colors exactly as specified
- ✅ Match typography style direction
- ✅ Preserve logo proportions and positions
- ✅ Follow the established grid/spacing
- ✅ Apply brand color mood (warm/cool/professional)
- ✅ Use approved imagery style

### DON'T:
- ❌ Deviate from brand colors without approval
- ❌ Mix in non-brand fonts
- ❌ Change logo placement
- ❌ Ignore spacing guidelines
- ❌ Use unapproved imagery styles
- ❌ Break the established visual hierarchy

---

## Step 3: Verification

Before delivering, verify:
- [ ] Colors match brand palette exactly
- [ ] Typography follows brand guidelines
- [ ] Logo appears in correct position
- [ ] Spacing matches reference
- [ ] Overall mood matches brand tone
- [ ] No unauthorized elements added
